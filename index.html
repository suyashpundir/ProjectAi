Ancient Civilizations

Civilizations like Mesopotamia, Ancient Egypt, Indus Valley, and China laid the foundations for writing, governance, architecture, and religion. These societies thrived thousands of years ago, showcasing early urban planning, agriculture, and mythology.

3000 BCE - 500 BCE Middle East, Asia

Roman Empire

One of the greatest empires in history, the Romans dominated Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia for centuries. Known for their engineering feats, legal system, military prowess, and contributions to art and architecture, the empire eventually fell in 476 AD in the West.

27 BCE - 476 CE Europe, North Africa, Asia

Middle Ages

Spanning roughly from the 5th to the 15th century, this period is marked by feudalism, the rise of the Catholic Church, the Crusades, and the Black Death. Often seen as a "dark" period, it also saw the foundations laid for modern Europe.

5th - 15th Century Europe

Renaissance

A cultural and intellectual rebirth beginning in 14th-century Italy, the Renaissance emphasized art, science, and humanism. It gave rise to iconic figures like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Galileo, and profoundly influenced European thought.

14th - 17th Century Europe

Industrial Revolution

Starting in the 18th century in Britain, the Industrial Revolution transformed economies with the rise of factories, machines, and urbanization. It sparked major social, economic, and environmental changes across the world.

18th - 19th Century Global
